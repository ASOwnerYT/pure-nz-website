Importance of freshwater habitats

Freshwater refers to water from rivers, lakes, reservoirs, underground streams, and other sources. Freshwater ecosystems contribute to biodiversity, the economy, recreational opportunities, cultural significance and our well-being. Freshwater is used by a wide variety of native plants and animals. Some of these are unique to New Zealand and often highly specialised to the habitats they are found in. Tangata whenua have strong links to water. Awa (river) are an important part of whakapapa (geneaology) and freshwater sustains taniwha and protects wāhi tapu (sacred areas). Awa also provide valuable resources such as mahinga kai (harvesting), harakeke (flax) and habitat for species of cultural importance (e.g. tuna/eels).
